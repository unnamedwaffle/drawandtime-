‚ú® Gesture Drawing Master Class

The line of action captures the main gesture and energy of your subject.

  • ‚Ä¢ Look for the dominant curve or line
  • ‚Ä¢ This line shows movement and flow
  • ‚Ä¢ Draw it first, everything else follows
  • ‚Ä¢ Can be curved, straight, or S-shaped

Finding the Line

  • ‚Ä¢ Squint to see the main flow
  • ‚Ä¢ Ignore details completely
  • ‚Ä¢ Look for spine/central axis
  • ‚Ä¢ Follow the energy direction

1. Line of Action (2-5 seconds)

Single flowing line capturing main gesture

2. Basic Shapes (10-15 seconds)

Head, torso, pelvis as simple shapes

3. Limbs & Movement (20-30 seconds)

Arms, legs following the gesture

4. Volume & Weight (60+ seconds)

Add dimension and weight distribution

üîß Troubleshooting Common Problems

Drawing Issues

üò´ "My drawings look stiff and lifeless"

Solution: Focus on gesture first. Start every drawing with a flowing line of action before adding any details.

üò§ "I run out of time before finishing"

Solution: Plan your time: 60% basic shape, 25% features, 15% details. Focus on silhouette recognition first.

üòî "My proportions are always wrong"

Solution: Use comparative measuring. Ask "how many times does this width fit into that height?" Compare sizes constantly.

Mental Blocks

üò∞ "I freeze up when the timer starts"

Solution: Start moving immediately. Draw any mark on the canvas within 3 seconds. Movement builds confidence.

üòï "I don't know what to draw for this prompt"

Solution: Draw the first thing that comes to mind. Don't overthink. Simple interpretations are often the most effective.

üò° "I keep erasing and starting over"

Solution: Commit to your first attempt. Erasing wastes time. Learn to work with "mistakes" - they often lead to better drawings.
